BERNADETTE FIGUEROA

DIGITAL PHOTOMONTAGE
DESIGNED BY ME
2018


"Modern Day Woman", 2018

For the Time Capsule project, I wanted to reflect on the major shift in post-war fashion due to Christian Dior’s “The New Look”
and how the style is still very relevant, focusing on the concept of “Timeless Fashion” and the ideal of a “Modern Day Woman”.
Fashion is a transparent representative of its era. Two years after the war, Christian Dior debuts his first collection, which was a
showstopper. Merely because during wartime women were wearing simple, straight and heavy dresses. They were dressed in structured
attire, merely because they couldn’t afford to do anything else extravagant. For Dior this was an ode to women, a return back to
glamour and femininity by accentuating the waist and emphasizing the bust with full skirts and constructed clothing to give the emphasis of the
hourglass shape. Women were badass and did what they had to do: taking over the civilian workforce and replacing once male-dominated
occupations. 4 million women working and 350,000 joining the military, which is remarkable. To me this was the start of the modern
day women narrative. So for this complete 360 was a breath of fresh air.

Seventy years later, we still see the full skirts and well thought out constructed dresses influenced by the 1950’s. When I saw
Maison Margiela’s Couture Artisanal A/W 2018 collection, I saw The New Look style intertwined with Margiela’s couture. The
wide-brimmed garden-style hats made of trash bag material, the clinched waist and accentuated hips on this one particular models’
outfit.

For me, it was the perfect mixture of the past and present. The stylized “New Look” mixed with Galliano’s “techno sorbet” color
palette and interesting choice of recycled materials. Then when I think about what a modern day woman is like today, I think of an
independent woman who has her priorities straightened and doesn’t let a man define her worth. She isn’t conflicted with her
self-esteem and does whatever she pleases. In the height of the #MeToo movement and women reclaiming the respect they deserve,
Margiela’s collection to me screams authenticity and full of vibrant colors.

In the composite I wanted to highlight the contrast that with the lack of colors they used back then. And since the composite is
supposed to be implanting a surrealistic collage technique, I thought it was only right to use a Dada/Surrealism photomontage style.
In my composite it’s highly influenced by Hannah Hoch’s style, it has that look of “oh I just cut and pasted the images on there”,
very avant garde. I didn’t want to do anything way too crazy because I really want the viewer to focus in on the compare and contrast
of the two outfits simultaneously. I repeated the model a few times to suggest movement. I added a few opposite colored dots on each
side to give a sense of it being each style being correlated. I wanted the design to look clean and organized personally so I didn’t
add any opposite colored dots on the models, I felt like it looked too messy. I believe that eyes are unintentionally become a focal
point, so I added sensors covering the eyes, in order for the viewer to really pay attention to the fashion and add some
mysteriousness. The background is a lovely shade of pink but also has the phrase repeated in typeface Didot “Modern Day Women”. These
two outfits to me represented the “Modern Day Women”, but evidently, in different times.
